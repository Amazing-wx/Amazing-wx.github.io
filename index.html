Project 1: National Undergraduate Training Program for Innovation and Entrepreneurship: Mechanical Design and Control of a New Traffic Directing Robot （Undergraduate period）

Duration: Sept. 2022—Jun. 2023

Description: To improve the intelligent efficiency and reduce the safety accidents of the traffic police operation, the combination of ‘robot’ and ‘traffic’ is used to design a mobile intelligent robot. It can work continuously for 24 hours to solve the safety problem of traffic police due to fatigue operation in the evening. In terms of mechanical structure, the adoption of the body + robotic arm + chassis three main parts of the composition.

1.Designed an intelligent traffic-directing robot based on an STM32 chip with two 7-DOF humanoid robotic arms and four McNamee wheels to move. It replaced humans to achieve the functions of directing traffic and movable electronic police.

2.Adjusted the PWM duty cycle to control the rotation angle of each servo, enabling the two 7-DOF robotic arms to simulate the command gestures of traffic police.

3.Utilized the camera mounted on arms for image recognition with bottom McNamee wheels, it acts as a mobile electronic police officer to capture violations of the law, making up for the limitations of the current electronic police officer's fixed position.

Achievement :- National First Prize of the 23rd China Robotics and Artificial Intelligence Competition, Dec.2021.\ - National First Prize of the International Youth Artificial Intelligence Innovation Competition, Nov.2021.

Project 2: Design and Motion Control of a Six-DOF Parallel Stabilized Platform for Offshore Wave Compensation （postgraduate stage）

Duration: Dec.2023 - Preseant Commissioned by China General Nuclear Power Group Shanwei Laboratory| Project Team Student Leader

Description: To improve the accuracy of offshore equipment, ensure the safety of operation and maintenance personne and increasing the window for offshore operations. Designed a 6-DOF Stewart parallel platform based on UCU mechanism with a 3-DOF gangway to compensate for wave motion.

1.Designed a two-tiered Stewart platform as an experimental prototype.The lower Stewart platform is used as a motion simulator geneartor to provide wave motion for a variety of sea states ,and the upper platform is kept smooth by controlling the length of each branch chain to isolate vibrations.

2.Proposed an improved linear active disturbance reject control (ILADRC) with linear quadratic regulator (LQR) based on joint space to precisely control the chain limb in the 6UCU Stewart parallel mechanism, thus keeping the top platform stable.

3.Utilized LQR to replace the series PD control in the traditional LADRC to solve the problem of controller bandwidth and its stability is verified using the Lyapunov theory. Used the LESO to estimate and compensate for the total disturbance of system

Achievement :- Wenxuan Wang, Yang Zhang, Peng Xu*, Bing Li*. Linear active disturbance rejection control with linear quadratic regulator for Stewart platform in active wave compensation system. Applied Ocean Research,2024 (JCR Q1).paper

Project 4: Hybrid variable stiffness collaborative robot for abrasive machining

Members: Zhisen Li, Wenxuan Wang, Chenghao Huang, Kun Chen, Shen Xu. Instructors::Bin Li, Peng Xu. ------ Dec.2023 - Sept.2024

Description: Designed a hybrid variable stiffness collaborative robot for grinding of complex curved components. The hybrid robot combines the high stiffness and high load capacity of parallel robots and the large workspace characteristics of tandem robots to perform a larger range of actions while maintaining structural stability.

1.A variable stiffness joint based on a rope-pulley mechanism is proposed with the goal of high compactness and high torque density. The compact and modular design of the joint structure optimises the space utilisation of the robot.

2.A novel two-rotation-one-translation 3-DOF redundant parallel end mechanism is proposed, and a lightweight structural design of the parallel end mechanism is carried out.

3.An improved ILADRC is proposed to compensate the position error caused by uncertain disturbance by introducing an exponential function to improve the performance of the actuator. A fuzzy logic loop is introduced into the adaptive term of the traditional adaptive impedance system, and a fuzzy adaptive conductance controller based on fuzzy inference rules is proposed to solve the force overshooting problem in the grinding force tracking process.

Achievement: National First Prize of the 6th Postgraduate Robot Innovation and Design Competition.

Project 5: A multifunctional robot for mine rehabilitation grass laying

Members: Wenxuan Wang, Hongchang Zhao, Chaolei Pang, Huipeng He, Tianchi Wang.------ June 2020 - June 2021

Description: Developed a robot for mine rehabilitation and landslide management that combines drilling, pouring, laying and spraying of grass seeds as an ecosystem restoration robot for mulching and vegetation.

1. Responsible for the design and machining of the overall mechanical structure, the animation of the model, the motion control of the chassis, and the drilling of the robotic arm.

2. Equipped with a mobile chassis with double tracks, it can adapt to the complex terrain of the mining area; utilized a hydraulic telescopic arm to control the auger for drilling work; used a seeding mechanism to spray grass seeds; and laid down grass squares by means of a belt conveyor mechanism.

Project 6: A new courier delivery robot based on triangular track drive

Members: Wenxuan Wang, Huipeng He, Chaolei Pang.------ June 2020 - June 2021

Description: Studied an intelligent courier delivery robot based on embedded management system can climb the stairs. Using the advantage of climbing stairs with triangular track wheels to solve the problem that today's delivery vehicles can not go up the stairs, followed by the use of a database to record the back-end logistics management information for the combination of hardware and software to achieve efficient distribution!

1. Combined the triangular wheels and track structure to solve the limitation of only being able to form on a flat ground; added a multi-degree-of-freedom robotic arm at the top of the vehicle to allow for automatic door opening.

2. Adopted the QR code real-name system to pick up express delivery: after the microcontroller detects the corresponding QR code information, it will feed back to the electromagnetic lock to open the door of the box, and at the same time, another data is retained in the database.

3. Responsible for the mechanical design of the robot, chassis motion control, and gripping action of the robotic arm.

Achievement :National First Prize of the 14th National 3D Digital Innovation Design Competition.

Project 7: National Engineering Training Comprehensive Ability Competition for College Students - Intelligent Logistics Handling Track

Memebers: Wenxuan Wang, Xiangseng Kong, Chaolei Pang, Huipeng He.------ Dec 2020 - July 2021

Description: Inside a given field, design a trolley that can autonomously complete material handling through a given task. By scanning the QR code to identify the sequence of logistics handling, the robot autonomously decides to carry the materials to the designated area.

1. Utilized the Openmv for the colour recognition of the logistics, and the corresponding colours are sorted and subsequently transmitted to the robotic arm via serial commands to complete the gripping action.

2. Arrange laser range finders in all four directions of the trolley. Calculate the size of the field and the feedback data from the laser rangefinders to establish a planar coordinate system for real-time positioning of the trolley.

4. Responsible for the mechanical structure design of the handling trolley, the colour recognition of Openmv, and the use of an upper computer to implement the gripping action of the robotic arm.

Achievement :Provincial Third Prize of National Engineering Training Comprehensive Ability Competition for College Students, July.2021.
